Introduction

In Information and Communication Technology (ICT), hand tools are essential for assembling, maintaining, and repairing computer hardware. Proper use of hand tools ensures safety, accuracy, and prevents damage to electronic components.

Proper use of these tools ensures safety, prevents damage to components, and improves efficiency during work. Understanding the function, definition, and proper handling of each tool helps students and professionals perform ICT tasks effectively while minimizing errors and hazards.

Different Hand Tools in ICT

Screwdriver

Definition: A tool used to turn screws.

Uses: Tightening and loosening screws when assembling or disassembling computer parts.

Long Nose Pliers

Definition: A tool with long, narrow jaws.

Uses: Holding small components and wires in tight spaces.

Wire Cutter

Definition: A cutting tool for wires.

Uses: Cutting electrical and network cables cleanly.

Crimping Tool

Definition: A tool used to attach connectors.

Uses: Attaching RJ45 connectors to LAN cables.

Anti-Static Wrist Strap

Definition: A safety device against static electricity.

Uses: Prevents damage to electronic components caused by static discharge.

Tweezers

Definition: A small tool for gripping tiny objects.

Uses: Picking up small screws and delicate electronic parts.

Phillips Screwdriver

Definition: A screwdriver with a cross-shaped tip.

Uses: Commonly used for computer screws such as motherboard and hard drive screws.

Flat Head Screwdriver

Definition: A screwdriver with a flat tip.

Uses: Used for loosening and tightening flat-head screws in computer cases and devices.

Torx Screwdriver

Definition: A screwdriver with a star-shaped tip.

Uses: Used for specialized screws found in laptops and hard drives.

LAN Tester

Definition: A device used to test network cables.

Uses: Checks if LAN cables are properly connected and working.

Punch Down Tool

Definition: A tool used to insert wires into terminals.

Uses: Used in network installation such as patch panels and keystone jacks.

Cable Stripper

Definition: A tool for removing insulation from wires.

Uses: Prepares network and electrical wires for crimping or connection.

Multimeter

Definition: An electronic measuring instrument.

Uses: Measures voltage, current, and resistance in ICT troubleshooting.

Screw Organizer

Definition: A container for organizing screws.

Uses: Keeps screws safe and prevents loss during computer assembly.

Cleaning Brush

Definition: A soft brush for cleaning electronics.

Uses: Removes dust from computer components without damaging them.
